Evangelion: 2.0 You Can (Not) Advance

THE STORY TAKES A BIG TURN

The first Rebuild movie stayed close to the original, but Evangelion: 2.0 changes things in a big way. New scenes, characters, and surprises make the story more exciting and unpredictable.

Released in 2009, this movie mixes familiar moments with completely new ideas. It's where the Rebuild series starts to feel very different from the original, keeping fans guessing and wanting more.

WHAT'S DIFFERENT IN EVANGELION: 2.0?

FAMILIAR... BUT TOTALLY DIFFERENT

Evangelion: 2.0 starts off feeling a lot like the original series, but then things take a sharp turn. New characters, like Mari, bring a fresh energy to the story, and even familiar characters like Asuka get some big changes that make their arcs feel new and exciting.

The story itself adds plenty of surprises. While some key moments from the original are still there, many are completely reimagined or replaced with new scenes. By the end, the movie has taken the story in a bold, unexpected direction, setting the Rebuild series apart as its own unique version of Evangelion.

Evangelion: 2.0 isn't just a retelling, it's where the Rebuild series really starts to feel like its own thing, keeping fans guessing every step of the way.

Mari Makinami

A NEW pilot's in town!

Mari Makinami, AKA the fifth child is on board! Mari Makinami is one of the biggest additions to the Rebuild series, and she's nothing like anyone we've seen before.

Confident, playful, and totally unpredictable, Mari brings a fresh vibe to the story. She's not weighed down by the same emotional struggles as the other pilots, which makes her stand out in a world full of heavy drama.

Mari isn't just a new pilot, she's a wild card, and her presence hints that the Rebuild series is heading into uncharted territory.
